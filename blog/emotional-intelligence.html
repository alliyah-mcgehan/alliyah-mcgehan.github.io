Emotional intelligence (EQ) refers to a person's ability to understand their own emotions as well as the emotions of others around them. On the contrary intelligence quotient (IQ) refers to a person's mental and logical capability. The three steps of emotional intelligence are mindfulness, self knowledge/self mastery and mental habits. Practising mindfulness can be helpful for improving attention and compassion. Self knowledge is useful for recognising one's emotions and learning to regulate them. Useful mental habits can help defend against the superego (critical or judgmental thoughts). The main benefits of strengthening one's emotional intelligence are stellar performance, outstanding leadership and happiness. Stellar performance is shown through conceptual thinking, analytical ability, initiative and self confidence. Emotional intelligence is important for leadership because it involves a deeper understanding of others.
