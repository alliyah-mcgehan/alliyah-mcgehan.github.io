Neuroplasticity allows the brain to rewire itself in response to experience, which helps to increase its resilience. Learning a second language, adequate sleep, and memorization techniques are examples of activities that may improve the neuroplasticity of your brain. Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity helps people to cultivate its benefits. If they are able to understand its principles they can implement them into their daily life and create positive changes for their brain. The principle “intensity matters” is relevant to me during this programme. Challenging tasks can lead to neuroplasticity and motor learning. JavaScript and all of its different concepts definitely stimulate my mind. The principle “use it and improve it” is also relevant to me because I need to stay consistent. Consistency will help me improve my skills and hopefully trigger neuroplasticity.

A growth mindset describes how a person views challenges and setbacks. A person with a growth mindset believes that their abilities aren't fixed, despite any struggles that they face. It is important to have this mindset because it motivates people to take risks without the fear of failing and boosts resilience. A person with this mindset can remain calm when they make mistakes, because they know it's an opportunity to learn and grow.
