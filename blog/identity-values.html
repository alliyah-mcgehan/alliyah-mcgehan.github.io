At my previous job I became close with a coworker, who I later found out was taking small amounts of money/products from the company. Since I had already become close with the coworker it was difficult to decide on how to go about the situation but I knew what they were doing was wrong. I decided to talk to them about it first, instead of going straight to management, which may be questionable to some people. They assured me that they rarely did it and that it was fine but eventually other co-workers began to notice. I decided to go management and speak to them about the situation. The coworker doesn't resent me and I don't regret going to them first, instead of management. I do not regret it because I know that generally they are a good person but they had just made some mistakes. My mother is Filipino so we were raised on catholic beliefs and are very people oriented. This helps me get along with most people, which makes it easier to work in a team. My limitations are just my time management skills.
